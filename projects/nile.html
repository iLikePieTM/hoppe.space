Code Name:

NILE

Novel Irrigation and Land use Efficiency.

Conventional agricultural methods overcompensate plant and soil needs through wasteful watering practices and excessive application of pesticides and fertilizers, leading to substantial environmental damage. This damage takes on various forms, including pollution via runoff, soil depletion, and the extinction of local pollinators.

This is the problem my five teammates and I chose to tackle for our senior capstone project. After much research, we settled on a backyard scale, cylindrical robotic system that will use a variety of sensor inputs to dynamically react to plant and soil conditions. In the end, our system will be able to autonomously care for plants from the moment they are needed until harvest.

To get updates on NILE's status and to see our work in more detail, visit our website!
