Code Name:

"Fly Like an Eagle"

The official NASA Space Grant club at Embry-Riddle Prescott.

EagleSat resulted from the collaboration of some amazing students and the support of NASA and Embry-Riddle. Our current mission, EagleSat II is a 3U CubeSat set to launch sometime around 2021 as part of NASA Space Grant. The spacecraft will carry dual scientific payloads designed to detect and track cosmic rays in addition to testing how radiation in space can affect computer memory.

As the Structures Team Lead is my job to ensure the spacecraft survives launch and orbit. To accomplish this task my team and I have designed and built a custom 3U CubeSat structure for the mission that is both lighter and more space efficient than any commercially available structure. We will take this structure and submit it to a bombardment of thermal, shock, and vibration tests to ensure it is ready to be sent to space. In addition to the structure, we will also implement a thermal control device to keep all systems within optimal temperature ranges in the vacuum of space.
